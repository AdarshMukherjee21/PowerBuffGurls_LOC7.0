Welcome to Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR)

What is CSR?

Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) is the practice of businesses taking responsibility for their social and environmental impact. Originally, CSR was about philanthropy and charity, but today, it has evolved into a broader concept that includes ethical business practices, sustainability efforts, and community engagement. CSR is about companies being accountable not just to their shareholders, but also to society and the planet.

Why is CSR Important?

  • Consumer Trust & Loyalty: 40% of consumers prefer brands that align with their values and act in society’s best interests.
  • Employee Engagement: 70% of millennials consider a company’s commitment to community when choosing a job.
  • Increased Profitability: Companies that embed CSR can boost their profits by up to 81% through increased market share, cost savings, and brand loyalty.
  • Sustainability & Risk Management: Addressing social and environmental concerns reduces legal risks, enhances reputation, and secures long-term success.
